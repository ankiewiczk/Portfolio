Visual Demonstration of Euclid's Algorithm

Example of Euclid's algorithm on 237 vs 150

This is a visual representation of how Euclid solved the greatest common denominator between two numbers by factoring in the squares that fit in a rectangle of length and width of the two factors. I use three rotating colors to highlight each iteration of the algorithm. I also scale the rectangles to be as big as possible given the window size. In future improvements, I would like to iterate through each step on a given mouseclick or maybe even add a slider so you can step through the algorithm forward and backward.
